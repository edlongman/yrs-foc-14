Information for Parents

What is the Festival of Code?

We want to prevent the isolation that often comes with learning to program, and create a network of young people supporting each other through learning and solving real world challenges.

The Festival of Code is run with the principles of community, open source and open data and is ultimately about finding and fostering every child driven to teach themselves how to code.

Young Rewired State is led by Emma Mulqueeny, who is a Mum of two, a team of DBS-checked employees, vetted volunteers and part-time staff.

Parent Attendance

  • 13 & UnderYou do not need to attend the weekday sessions, but you are required to come to Plymouth on the weekend with your child.
  • 14 - 17You do not need to attend the weekday sessions or come down to Plymouth, but we will require you to sign a permission slip that we'll send out before the week begins.
  • 18You do not need to attend or give permission for any part of the event, but you are still welcome to come by.

Donations

If you'd like to donate either your time or funds to the Festival of Code, we thank you. We will accept all offers of sponsorship or donation however big or small, please contact emma@rewiredstate.org for the official channels for donation. We run the Festival at no profit, all funds are put towards the cost of the festival itself and the hardship fund for the families unable to support the necessary travel costs.

We will use this site to advertise the various volunteering opportunities for the Festival, so do check out this section of the website to see if there is anything you feel able to volunteer for.

Parent FAQ

  • How much does it cost?

    It's compeltely free. The only expense you expect you to make is travel to and from the centre and food. Some centres will provide food, some will have a cafeteria to buy lunch, and other will expect participants to bring their own lunch with them. You'll find out more closer to the event.

  • Is there help with the cost of travel?

    Yes. We have a hardship fund that can be applied to to cover the cost of travel. Forms for this will be sent in advance of the week.

  • How will I get my child to Plymouth?

    The centres will organise group travel either by train, plane, car, or coach. This is organised by your centre lead, but you are welcome to make alternate plans separate from the centre.

  • Do you teach kids how to code?

    No, we foster children who have already learnt how to code. We believe in peer-to-peer learning so inspire them to learn and then give them a community of peers and mentors to help them teach themselves, and real-world challenges to address to test and build their skills and knowledge.
