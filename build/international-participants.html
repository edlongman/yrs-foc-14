Information for International Participants

What to Bring

  • Your passport, but if you manage to get to the UK, we'll assume you have it.
  • A laptop with UK adaptor. Please install your prefered development environment before the week so you can get straight to hacking and not waste any time. You can write any languages or use any tools you like.
  • Spending money for breakfast and lunch. Dinner will be provided to you at no cost throughout the week.
  • You'll have access to showers, so please remember to bring toiletries (shampoo, soap, hower gel, etc) or money to purchase them when you're here.
  • If you're old school and want to use a phone to chat to your parents while you are over here, make sure you have a prepaid SIM card as well.

Parental Permission for Plymouth

  • 13 & UnderA parent must be with you for the whole week
  • 14 - 17You will need a signed permission slip which we will provide to you before the week. We would ideally like this back on the first day of the week.
  • 18No permission required

Taking Part Remotely

You are able to take part without coming to the UK if you'd prefer. Like everyone else, you will be creating a project utilising open data. You will be able to present to our panel of judges over Skype and our social channels are there if you need help.

International Participant FAQ

  • Do I need a Passport?

    Everyone will need a passport to attend the Festival of Code unless they are living in the UK or are an EEA citizen, in which you will only need your national identity card.

  • Will I need a visa?

    No visa is required if you have an America, EU, or Singapore passport. If you are travelling from a different country than those listed, please check your county's requirements for travelling abroad.

  • Will you pay for my flight?

    Unfortuntaley we cannot pay for your flight or book your travel arrangements. You can view our travel suggestions on our venue page, but it is ultimately up to you which method of travel you choose.
